# Ledger and Tracked Proxy live in one module

The **Ledger** (formerly `src/ledger.ts`) and the **Tracked Proxy** (formerly `src/tracked-proxy.ts`) are merged into a single internal module `src/tracked-data.ts` exporting one factory, `createTrackedData`. The split was attractive on paper — keeping the **Ledger** Vue-free as a structural property — but no consumer or test ever reached for a Vue-free **Ledger** in isolation, and the boundary was paying ongoing maintenance rent rather than catching anything. Worse, the bump-after-record obligation (call `triggerRef` after every `ledger.record`) was implicit and lived in the composable, making it easy to drift: a future caller adding a new mutation site would have to remember to fire the trigger, with no compile-time signal if they forgot. The two pieces are inseparable in practice — every **Path** that lands in the **Ledger** comes from a Proxy trap, every Proxy trap must bump the **Ledger**'s reactivity, and the **Array.length** synthesis dance only makes sense when the trap and the **Ledger** can be reasoned about side-by-side. Folding them together concentrates the **Array.length** index synthesis, **Path** tracking, and the reactivity trigger in one place; `useTrackedInstance` shrinks to a `{ root: Data }` wrapper plus type re-exports, and the bump callback ceases to be part of the API.
